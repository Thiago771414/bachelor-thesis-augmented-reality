## Overview

This bachelor thesis explores Augmented Reality (AR) systems and their applications across education, industry, and human interaction environments.  
The research investigates adoption trends, major ecosystem investments, and the relationship between technology production and natural resource sustainability.

## Research Objectives

- Analyze AR software ecosystems and market adoption
- Evaluate AR impact on creativity development in children (education context)
- Compare AR research signals in developed vs emerging economies
- Investigate sustainability implications of large-scale technology production

## Methodology

- Literature review (Scielo, CAPES, journals and large-circulation sources)
- Comparative analysis across international contexts
- Review of AR ecosystem evolution and investment signals
- Sustainability framing based on resource consumption and regeneration constraints

## Key Questions

- Can AR-based learning environments influence creativity development in children?
- What are the sustainability tradeoffs of accelerating technology production?
- How do different economies balance technological growth and resource preservation?

## Highlights (Why it matters)

Even though this thesis was written in 2018, it anticipated trends that continue to accelerate:

- Spatial computing and AR ecosystem expansion  
- Education transformation through immersive interfaces  
- Systems-level thinking connecting tech growth and sustainability constraints
