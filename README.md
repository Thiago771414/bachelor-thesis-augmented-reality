# Augmented Reality Systems — Undergraduate Research (2018)

## Overview

This bachelor thesis explores Augmented Reality (AR) systems and their applications across education, industry, and human interaction environments.

The research investigates technological adoption trends, global investment patterns (including major ecosystem players such as Apple), and the socio-environmental implications of large-scale technology production.

---

## Research Objectives

- Analyze core AR software ecosystems and market adoption
- Evaluate AR impact on creativity development in children
- Compare AR adoption in developed vs emerging economies
- Investigate the relationship between technological production and natural resource sustainability

---

## Methodology

- Literature review (Scielo, CAPES, international journals)
- Comparative international research analysis
- Technology investment evaluation
- Sustainability and resource consumption assessment

---

## Key Research Questions

- Does augmented reality influence creativity development in children?
- How does large-scale technology production impact natural resources?
- Are developed countries balancing technological growth and sustainability?

---

## Strategic Relevance

Although conducted in 2018, this research anticipated key trends that are now accelerating:

- Spatial computing
- AR ecosystem growth
- Educational technology transformation
- Sustainable technology production

This project reflects early systems-level thinking that later evolved into professional work in distributed systems architecture and scalable backend engineering.
